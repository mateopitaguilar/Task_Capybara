CAPYABARAS

Introduction

Capybaras are the largest rodents in the world. They are known for their friendly and social nature. Capybaras are native to South America and are often found near water bodies. They have a unique ability to bond with other animals, making them quite special.

Interesting facts about Capybaras

  • Capys are excellent swimmers.
    1. They can stay underwater for up to 5 minutes.
    2. They have webbed feet, this adaptation helps them swim efficiently.
  • They live in groups.
    1. Groups can consist of 10-20 individuals.
  • Capybaras communicate through a variety of sounds.
    1. They use barks, whistles, and purrs to communicate.

Capybara diet

  1. Grasses
    • They prefer aquatic plants.
  2. Fruits
    • They enjoy eating melons and squashes.
